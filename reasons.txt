### Build Breakdown
- **Timeline**: **Completed in ~2 Hours** (from napkin sketch to production-ready MVP).
- **Automation & Efficiency**: 
  - Scaffolding: Next.js 14 + shadcn/ui for rapid design.
  - Transformation: Automated the mapping of complex YouTube metadata to Recharts-ready formats.
- **Problem Solving**: When initial testing revealed "0" view values, I implemented a validation script to diagnose the playlist API limitation and successfully pivoted to a search-based extraction method.
- **Verification**: Integrated `Vitest` for automated regression testing, successfully validated against high-frequency channels like **MIT OpenCourseWare**.
- **Configuration**: Migrated all hardcoded URLs to `.env` for demo portability and production readiness.

### Product Thinking
- **Metric Calibration**: Chose to prioritize "Monthly Reach" as the primary KPI to align with the founder's goal of seeing "who is crushing it *this month*".
- **Temporal context**: Added "NEW" badges to distinguish recent uploads from the channel's evergreen library.
- **Missing Features**: Real-time thumbnail A/B patterns, comment sentiment analysis, and multi-channel stack ranking.
- **Version 2 Plan**: 
  - Sub-niche clustering using NLP on video titles.
  - Historical view velocity (how fast is it growing *today*?).
  - Slack/Email digest of competitor uploads.

### Trade-offs
- Chose `youtube-sr` over official API to ship faster and avoid quota issues during the challenge demo.
- Prioritized "Visual Reach" (Total Views/Avg) over engagement depth (Like/Comment ratios) for the initial napkin sketch MVP.
